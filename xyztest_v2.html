Previously I managed the application lifecycle for General Motors’ global applications until I left to attend Makers Academy, a software developer bootcamp, where I learned even more about software design.

BIG RESPONSIBILITIES

Let's build great products. And then make them better.

It looks kind of important, doesn't it?

  • From GM to Makers Academy, I’ve worked on projects big and small and I love product development. From inception to wireframes, development, testing, deployment, post-deployment improvements, I love building products people use and then improving them.

    [NYT / Richard Pérez-Peña]

  • I have managed complex projects, prioritizing day-to-day technical tasks alongside continuous improvements.

    [Vox / German Lopez]

  • Have regularly debugged product issues; reported, tracked, and prioritized bugs that needed immediate developer attention.

    [Vox / German Lopez]

  • Have translated business requirements to technical requirements.

    [CNN / Ralph Ellis, Don Melvin, and David Shortell]

MAD SKILLS

Damn, can I learn quickly. [YouTube / Makers Academy Visitors App]

  • Have used analytics to improve products and the user experience.

    [GM Accessories Zone / GMNA]

  • Data analysis with SQL(Postgres) and Excel.

  • Hacky copy/paste HTML+CSS jobs.

    [This Site / Milena]

BONUS POINTS

  • Over 3 years' experience working remotely with both clients and distributed developer teams.

    [ General Motors / North America, Europe, Australia]

  • Write personal blog on life, travels, food, failed cooking attempts, and often include pictures of other people's pets.
